The draw completes, the ticket number is called, and somewhere a phone is about to ring. Here's what to expect from that moment through to having the car — or the cash — in your hands.

## Immediately after the draw

Most operators contact the winner within minutes to a couple of hours of the draw. The call or email comes from the operator directly. For live-streamed draws, the ticket number is announced on-screen before anyone is contacted.

**Do not assume you've been forgotten if you don't hear immediately.** Draws sometimes complete outside office hours. Operators may need to verify the ticket against their database before making contact. A delay of a few hours is normal; a delay of days is unusual and worth raising with the operator.

If you think you may have won and haven't been contacted: find the draw recording (most are posted to Facebook/YouTube within hours), note the winning ticket number, and check it against your tickets. If it's yours and you haven't heard within 24 hours, contact customer support directly.

## Identity verification

Before any prize is released, you'll be asked to verify your identity. Standard requirements are:

- **Photo ID** — passport or driving licence
- **Proof of address** — a bank statement or utility bill, typically dated within the last 3 months
- **Confirmation of eligibility** — 18+, UK resident, not a prohibited person (some operators exclude company directors and employees)

For higher-value prizes, some operators require an affidavit or statutory declaration — a signed, witnessed statement confirming your identity and eligibility. Some operators may require additional checks above certain prize values. Read the T&Cs before you enter, not after.

## The cash alternative

Almost every operator offers a cash alternative to the car. The amount varies — it's typically the prize value as advertised minus a percentage (in practice, the cash figure is usually 60–90% of the car's headline value, depending on operator and competition; verify in the T&Cs for the specific competition).

**Most winners take the cash.** The practical reasons are obvious: no insurance to sort, no delivery to arrange, no depreciation clock ticking. The tax position is the same either way — tax-free.

Cash is paid directly to your bank account. Dream Car Giveaways and Click Competitions are known for fast payment — some documented cases are within 24 hours. Budget a few days for the bank transfer to clear regardless.

## Receiving the car

If you take the car, the operator arranges delivery or collection. Logistics vary:

- **New cars:** sourced from a dealer and delivered to you, or you collect from a dealer the operator nominates. Timeline depends on stock — common models in stock can arrive within a week; low-production-run cars may take longer.
- **Cash-purchase cars already sourced:** occasionally operators have already purchased the prize car and it can be delivered relatively quickly.

Budget 1–4 weeks for vehicle delivery as a reasonable range, though some operators have documented delivery within days. Rev Comps, in particular, has a large archive of winner stories with delivery timelines.

The car will be registered in your name. You're responsible for insurance from the point of delivery. Sort it in advance — you'll need a policy before you can drive it away.

## Tax status

Prize winnings from UK competitions are **not subject to income tax or capital gains tax**. HMRC's position is that prizes are not earnings and are not trading income, so the prize comes to you tax-free.

If you subsequently sell the car, and it has increased in value above what you received it at, normal capital gains tax rules apply to the profit — but that's a future event, not the prize itself. In practice, most cars depreciate.

## A few things to do when you win

1. **Don't announce publicly before the operator does.** Most operators want to control the announcement (they have their own social media content to post). Jumping ahead can complicate the verification process.
2. **Get the prize confirmed in writing** before you make any financial commitments based on it.
3. **Read the terms before signing anything.** Specifically: the cash alternative amount, any conditions on vehicle use, and the timeline commitments the operator is making.
4. **If you're taking the car:** check the spec matches what was advertised before accepting delivery. Minor discrepancies do happen.
