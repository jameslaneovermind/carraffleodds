Lucky Day Competitions is run by Ace Competitions Limited, incorporated in Northern Ireland in 2019. The brand was built around its founder Sammy Heaney, who became a public figure in the agricultural and farming media world through the associated social media presence. In November 2025, Sammy stepped back from her director role, with Sabrina McKeever taking over. The reasons for the change haven't been publicly explained, and the brand's direction under the new director isn't yet clear.

## How it works

Fixed-odds draws with a skill question. Most standard competitions are 97p per ticket, with prices ranging from 10p to £24.97. Three draw types: live Facebook draws using Google's Random Number Generator (with ticket numbers visible), auto draws triggered on sellout or timer expiry, and instant wins where every ticket gets a notification immediately. At least one winner is guaranteed per draw.

**Free entry:** Postal entry to their Newry address — handwritten, with your competition name, full name, address, contact details, and the skill question answer. Hand-delivered entries aren't accepted.

## What's on offer

Cars (BMWs, Land Rovers, Toyota Supra, VW Tiguan R-Line, Toyota Hilux), agricultural equipment (tractors, excavators, quad bikes), cash prizes (typically £500–£20,000+, tax-free), tech, and holidays. The farming and agricultural prize niche is genuinely unique — no other UK competition site we track offers tractors or farm machinery as prizes. Cash alternatives are available for all prizes.

## Trust signals

- Incorporated 2019, 7+ years of operation

- GDPR-compliant, 3D Secure 2 payment authentication
- Large social following (291,947 Facebook likes; 73,000 Instagram followers)
- Long draw archive available

## Honest watch points

**No responsible gambling messaging.** Lucky Day has no Begambleaware integration, no account deposit limits, and no cooling-off options visible on the site. That's a gap compared to operators who take this more seriously.

**Director transition.** Sammy Heaney stepped back from her director role in November 2025. She was the public face of the brand and its agricultural identity. It's not yet clear how involved she remains, or how the brand's direction changes under the new director. Worth monitoring.

**"Remaining" vs. "sold" tickets.** Lucky Day shows remaining tickets rather than tickets sold, which makes it harder to calculate how many have been entered. Most operators show tickets sold. It's a small thing but affects your ability to assess real odds at any given moment.

**Voluntary Code status.** Lucky Day hasn't been confirmed as a signatory to the DCMS Voluntary Code of Good Practice.

## Who it suits

Anyone interested in agricultural or rural prizes won't find a better option — the tractor and farming equipment competitions are genuinely distinctive. For standard car competitions, compare across [Elite Competitions](/sites/elite-competitions) and [7 Days Performance](/sites/7-days-performance) too. Northern Irish and Irish entrants appear in community forums more often than you'd expect by population size, but there's no public data to draw firm conclusions from.
