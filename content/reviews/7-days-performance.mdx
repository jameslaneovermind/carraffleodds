7 Days Performance has run weekly draws every Monday at 8 PM since 2018, focused on performance and supercar prizes — sports BMWs, Audis, Porsches, McLarens, and Lamborghinis, alongside modified and tuned cars. Every draw is guaranteed to produce a winner regardless of how many tickets sell. That's a meaningful commitment: no rollovers, no extensions, someone always wins on Monday.

## How it works

Fixed-odds draw with a multiple-choice skill question. Ticket prices run from 20p to £1, with most main competitions at around 50p; bulk discounts are available. Ticket count is displayed before you buy, which means you can calculate your actual odds yourself rather than taking the site's word for it. Draws run live every Monday at 8 PM on Facebook and YouTube, using a certified random number generator.

**Free entry:** Daily free draw via the mobile app, with no purchase required. Postal entry is also available. Note that the free draw prizes are smaller than the paid competitions, and community reports suggest postal entries rarely win the main draws.

## What's on offer

Prize focus is performance and supercar: Lamborghini Aventador, McLaren 600LT and 720S, Porsche GT3 RS, Ferrari 488, plus tuned BMWs, Audis, and Mercedes G63. Standard draws include BMW M3s, Ford Transits, and Suzuki Jimnys. Cash alternatives are typically £120k–£170k, tax-free.

## Honest watch points

**Addiction concerns.** 7 Days Performance comes up frequently in MoneySavingExpert and PistonHeads forums as a platform people find difficult to stop using. The weekly Monday draw creates a strong habit loop. This doesn't make the company disreputable, but it's worth being honest with yourself about spending before you get into a routine.

**Geographic winner distribution.** Community members have noted a perceived concentration of winners in South West England. 7 Days Performance hasn't addressed this publicly and there's no independent data to verify it, but it's a recurring forum discussion worth knowing about.

**Ticket sold count per draw.** The total number of tickets available is shown, but the number sold isn't always visible, which limits your ability to calculate real-time odds rather than maximum odds.
