Click Competitions launched in April 2020 — the most recently founded site in our dataset — and was acquired by Winvia Entertainment PLC in April 2025 for an undisclosed sum. Winvia also owns [BOTB](/sites/botb) and is acquiring [Rev Comps](/sites/rev-comps), so once the Rev Comps deal closes, three of the eight sites we track will share the same parent. The founders both stepped down on the day of acquisition.

Click's standout feature is payout speed: cash prizes are typically paid within 24 hours, against a stated guarantee of 21 calendar days. For a sector where prize delivery timelines vary considerably, that's a meaningful differentiator.

## How it works

Fixed-odds draw with a trivia question. Ticket prices run from 10p to £9.95, with most competitions at 29p–£1.99. There's a per-person cap of 100 entries online (600 by post). Draws run live on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Saturdays, streamed on Facebook and Instagram with Google RNG — ticket numbers visible. All draws are guaranteed on their scheduled date with no extensions.

**Free entry:** Postal entry available (handwritten, standard industry terms). Same odds as paid entries.

A note on the trivia question: answers must be correct or your entry is void. Read the question. It's a low bar but a few negative reviews mention entries being voided through inattention.

## What's on offer

Prize range is broader than most: cars (Cupra Leon VZ3, VW Golf GTD, performance vehicles), cash (up to £100k+), tech (Xbox, DJI drones, Breitling watches), and lifestyle prizes. The mix is deliberately wider than performance-car-specialist sites, which suits people who want options beyond the automotive niche.

## Honest watch points

**Shortest track record in the sector.** Founded in 2020, Click has five years of operating history versus 19+ for Elite Competitions and 26+ for BOTB. The Winvia acquisition adds institutional stability, but the independent track record is shorter.

**Instant wins without free-play option.** The instant win format is more gambling-like than a standard draw, and there's no free-play testing mechanism. Worth noting if that kind of mechanic concerns you.

**Responsible gambling features.** Not prominently featured in public-facing materials. Worth checking the account settings for any limits or cooling-off options if that matters to you.

## Who it suits

Click is a good option if fast payouts matter, if you want a Winvia-backed operator with more frequent draw days than weekly, or if you're interested in a broader prize mix beyond cars. Compare against [Elite Competitions](/sites/elite-competitions) on prize range and against [7 Days Performance](/sites/7-days-performance) on performance car focus. If you're already entering BOTB, it's worth knowing you'd be entering two Winvia-owned sites simultaneously.
