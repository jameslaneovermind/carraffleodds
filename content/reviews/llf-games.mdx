LLF Games specialises in rare, modified, and classic cars — a niche no other UK competition site covers in the same depth. Think 1968 Dodge Chargers, Honda NSXs, Ford Escorts with 2JZ swaps, and 90s Japanese legends rather than the new BMWs and Range Rovers you see everywhere else. It's run by a small team (three employees as of 2025) and is directly connected to the [Living Life Fast](https://www.youtube.com/@LivingLifeFast) automotive YouTube channel, which has 453,000 subscribers.

## How it works

Fixed-odds draws with a skill question for legal compliance. Entry prices run from 1p to £20 — the ultra-low end (1p–5p per ticket) is common for car draws. Draws are streamed live on Facebook, Instagram, and YouTube using Google RNG, with ticket numbers visible. Results are published on a dedicated results page with winner names and ticket numbers.

**Free entry:** Postal entry by handwritten postcard to their Bournemouth address. One entry per postcard; must exactly match your account details. Computer-generated entries are rejected.

## What's on offer

The prize selection is genuinely unusual for the sector. Recent and current competitions have included a 1968 7.2L V8 Dodge Charger, Honda NSX, BMW M140i, Toyota GT86, and various modified track cars — alongside more standard cash prizes. If you're looking for a Lamborghini or a new BMW M4, you'll find those elsewhere; if you're a petrolhead who wants a chance at a car with an actual story, this is your best option.

## Important concerns — read before entering

There are a few things worth knowing before you commit money here.

**Trustpilot rating flagged.** Trustpilot has flagged LLF Games for potential improper review solicitation. The 4.9 star score should be treated with more caution than usual because of this — it may not reflect the full picture.

**Responsible gambling concerns.** Reports on forums suggest that requests for addiction support have not been handled well by the company. There is no Begambleaware integration and no obvious responsible gambling messaging on the site. If you're at all concerned about your spending habits, this is relevant.

**Very high ticket volumes on some draws.** Some competitions have issued 1.6 million tickets at 2p each for an £8,000 prize — the expected value per ticket in that scenario is poor. Always check the total ticket count and prize value before entering.

**Not a Voluntary Code signatory.** LLF Games has not signed the DCMS Voluntary Code of Good Practice, which most of the larger operators have. That code covers things like responsible gambling messaging and draw transparency.

## Who it suits

Petrolheads who specifically want rare, modified, or classic cars and can't find what they're looking for anywhere else. If you do enter, stick to draws where the ticket economics make sense and the prize genuinely interests you. Avoid entering habitually.
