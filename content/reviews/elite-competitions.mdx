Elite Competitions has been running fixed-odds prize draws since 2006 — nearly two decades, which puts it firmly in the established tier of UK competition operators. Based in Blackpool (with a registered office in Essex), it has one of the larger review footprints of any site in the sector: 15,800+ Trustpilot reviews is a meaningful sample. Draws run live on Tuesday, Wednesday, and Friday at 9 PM, streamed on the website and social channels, so you can watch the result in real time.

## How it works

Each competition has a fixed number of tickets at a set price. You buy tickets, answer a skill question (required by UK law to operate outside Gambling Commission regulation), and wait for the draw. If you win, you're contacted within 30 minutes.

**Free entry:** You can post an open postcard to their Blackpool address at no cost. The postcard must name the specific competition, confirm you accept the T&Cs, and include your contact details.

## What's on offer

The prize range covers cars, cash, holidays, watches, and tech. All prizes are delivered free of charge. Notable wins include Nathan Conway (21 years old, £1,000,000 from the Million Vault competition) and Tony Holmes (a £1.2 million Christmas bundle including two Lamborghinis, a house, and £50k cash).

## Honest watch points

One recurring complaint on Trustpilot: draw dates get pushed back when a competition hasn't sold enough tickets. This happens across the industry — operators need enough revenue to cover the prize cost — but it can be frustrating if you expected a result on a specific date. It's worth knowing before you plan around a draw date.

## Who it suits

Elite is a solid, long-running choice if you want a broad prize range, three live draws a week, and a large body of reviews to gauge trust. If you're specifically after performance or rare cars, [7 Days Performance](/sites/7-days-performance) or [LLF Games](/sites/llf-games) are more specialist. If track record and review volume are your main criteria, Elite is near the top of the field.
